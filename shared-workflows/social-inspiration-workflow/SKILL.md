# Social Inspiration Workflow

Do not doomscroll for inspiration.
Build inputs that can be converted into topics.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the user wants to:
- find topic inspiration from social platforms
- avoid the "I have no ideas" state
- collect real reader language, not invented pain points
- turn comments, posts, threads, or discussions into公众号 topics
- identify what people are repeatedly anxious about, misunderstanding, or trying to explain

## Core Principle

Social platforms are not primarily for copying content.
They are for collecting:
- repeated anxieties
- lived scenes
- emotionally sticky phrasing
- mistaken explanations
- judgment lines worth reacting to

Your job is to convert these into:
- topics
- title directions
- opening hooks
- follow-up series ideas

## Best Input Sources

### Source Type 1: Reader-Language Platforms

Use these when the goal is real phrasing and emotional truth:
- comment sections
- forums
- Q&A threads
- short-post discussions
- creator replies and reader follow-ups

Extract:
- direct quotes that sound like real life
- repeated complaint patterns
- the exact way people describe their own stuckness

### Source Type 2: Judgment Platforms

Use these when the goal is better framing:
- strong creator essays
- long threads with sharp takes
- high-quality opinion posts
- experienced operators discussing work, growth, or burnout

Extract:
- strong reframes
- better language for naming a problem
- useful contrasts

### Source Type 3: Case Platforms

Use these when the goal is concrete reality:
- transition stories
- burnout posts
- work diaries
- long comment chains about a shared problem

Extract:
- life scenes
- turning points
- what people thought was wrong vs what was actually wrong

## Collection Rules

When reviewing social content, do not save everything.
Only save items that meet at least one of these:
- highly recognizable
- emotionally precise
- socially shareable
- reveals a repeated misunderstanding
- can be turned into a strong title or opening

Ignore:
- empty motivation
- recycled platitudes
- trend noise with weak relevance
- content that is viral but not aligned with the user's writing direction

## Extraction Framework

For each useful item, capture these fields:
1. platform
2. source type
3. original line or summary
4. what real-life state it reveals
5. what wrong explanation it suggests
6. what deeper theme it belongs to
7. possible公众号 topic
8. possible title line
9. possible opening scene

## Conversion Framework

After collecting signals, sort them into these buckets:
- high-recognition topic ideas
- title lines worth adapting
- opening scenes worth reusing
- repeated user-language patterns
- series-worthy themes

Then convert them into:
- 3 to 10 topic ideas
- top title candidates
- opening directions
- one recommended priority order

## Platform-Aware Guidance

For公众号 inspiration, prefer content that gives you:
- a better first screen
- a stronger recognition path
- more socially transmissible lines
- realistic work-life scenes

Do not simply lift hot takes from social platforms into公众号.
Translate them into deeper, calmer, more durable writing.

## Default Output Format

1. signal summary
2. repeated anxieties and language patterns
3. best topic opportunities
4. best title directions
5. best opening scenes
6. recommendation: what to write first

## Fast Filters

If the user says "I have no ideas," prioritize finding:
- repeated frustrations
- repeated self-misdiagnoses
- repeated comparison anxiety
- repeated work-life exhaustion language

If the user says "I want better titles," prioritize finding:
- strong contrast lines
- mistaken-identity framing

If the user says "I want better openings," prioritize finding:
- real-life scenes
- emotional trigger moments
- exact moments when the reader feels the problem

## Good Output Example

A strong result should sound like:

- many threads describe having "time on paper" but no usable energy
- this can become a公众号 topic about why adults are not short on time, but short on usable attention

Not like:

## Constraints

- Do not recommend copying another creator's framing too closely.
- Do not confuse virality with fit.
- Do not produce topic ideas with no lived texture.
- Do not stop at collection; always convert signals into usable writing assets.


## Phenomenon Mode

Use this mode when the input is not platform content but a real-life observation.

Typical inputs:
- a scene from daily life
- a sentence someone said
- a repeated work pattern
- a strange reaction or contradiction
- a small moment that feels more meaningful than it first appears

This mode is for turning:
- observations
- scenes
- behaviors
- micro-stories
- workplace friction

into:
- mechanisms
- reframes
- writeable公众号 topics

### Core Principle
A phenomenon is not yet a topic.
Treat it first as a clue.
Before turning it into content, first ask what mechanism it reveals.

Do not rush from observation to article.
Move through three layers:
1. capture the phenomenon clearly
2. expand the possible mechanisms behind it
3. derive multiple topic directions from the strongest mechanism

### Phenomenon Workflow

#### Stage 1: Capture
First capture the observation without over-explaining it.
Clarify:
1. what exactly happened
2. what felt strange, sharp, or recognizable
3. what the people involved seemed to want, avoid, protect, or misunderstand
4. why this scene stayed in the user's mind

At this stage, do not force a moral or lesson.
Just preserve the friction.

#### Stage 2: Mechanism Expansion
Use brainstorm / ideation / thought-partner style reasoning.
Generate several plausible deeper mechanisms behind the phenomenon.
Examples:
- self-serving bias
- defensive interpretation
- role-based misunderstanding
- status protection
- responsibility avoidance
- hidden incentives
- experience not being upgraded into judgment

Then evaluate:
- which mechanism is most emotionally precise
- which mechanism is most durable, not just situational
- which mechanism best fits the user's writing direction
- which mechanism has the strongest reframe potential

Do not stop at the first plausible explanation.
Expand first, then choose.

#### Stage 3: Topic Derivation
Once the strongest mechanism is selected, derive topic options.
Always generate several topic directions, not just one.
Prefer a spread such as:
- one high-recognition version
- one sharper diagnosis version
- one version that best fits公众号
- one version that best fits the user's positioning
- one version that could work as a sequel or short content spinoff

For each derived topic, state:
1. what is new about it
2. what pain point or misunderstanding it hits
3. whether it is better as an引流题, 主文题, 承接题, or 短内容题

### Output Format For Phenomenon Mode
When running in phenomenon mode, return:
1. phenomenon summary
2. possible mechanisms behind it
3. strongest mechanism and why
4. 3 to 5 topic directions
5. which topic is best for公众号
6. which topic best fits the user's positioning
7. which topic is most worth writing first

### Rule For This User
For this user, prefer mechanisms that can connect back to:
- judgment
- expression
- growth structure
- execution-layer stagnation
- experience upgrade
- second growth curve
- hidden friction in technical work and collaboration

That means a life observation should not stay as a general life lesson if it can be translated into:
- a workplace mechanism
- a growth misunderstanding
- a reusable公众号 topic line

### Important Constraint
Do not confuse "this is an interesting phenomenon" with "this should be published as-is".
Some observations are best used as:
- openings
- metaphors
- scene seeds
- side examples

Only elevate it into a main topic when the underlying mechanism is strong enough.

## Modes

Default mode is `positioning mode`.

### Positioning Mode (default)
Use this mode unless the user explicitly asks for hot topics, trends, or recent platform-wide discussions.

In positioning mode, the workflow must:
- prioritize the user's long-term content positioning over general platform popularity
- search for signals related to the user's audience, recurring anxieties, lived scenes, and misdiagnosed problems
- optimize for reusable topic inspiration, titles, openings, and series extensions
- treat heat or virality only as a secondary signal

For this user, default positioning should prioritize topics related to:
- work anxiety
- growth stagnation
- career transition and uncertainty
- technical professionals or ordinary office workers under long-term pressure
- people who want long-term accumulation but are repeatedly depleted by daily life

### Trend Mode (optional)
Only use this mode when the user explicitly asks to chase trends, hot topics, recent debates, or platform-wide momentum.

In trend mode, the workflow may:
- prioritize recent and high-discussion platform topics
- identify which trends overlap with the user's positioning
- filter out trends that are high-heat but low-fit for the user's account

### Mode Selection Rule
If the user does not specify a mode, always run in `positioning mode`.
Do not silently switch to trend mode based on your own judgment.

## Platform Priority

Use a layered platform strategy.

### Primary platforms
Use these as the main sources for reliable topic extraction:
- Zhihu
- Huxiu
- WeChat public account articles

These platforms are preferred because they are more likely to provide:
- indexable public pages
- fuller context and argument structure
- stable signals for repeated anxieties, framing, and topic packaging

### Secondary platforms
Use these as supporting sources, not primary evidence:
- Weibo
- Xiaohongshu

These platforms are useful for:
- hot phrases and short-lived discussion spikes
- lived scenes and colloquial language
- emotional cues and socially contagious wording

But they are less reliable for:
- stable public crawling
- complete post retrieval
- repeatable automation workflows

## Trend Window

When running in `trend mode`, default to the most recent 30 to 90 days of publicly searchable discussion signals.

If the user explicitly asks for a shorter window such as "this week", "these days", or "latest", narrow the search window accordingly.

Do not treat trend mode as a real-time hot-search scraper by default.
Instead, treat it as a workflow for finding recent, public, positioning-relevant discussion momentum.

## Trend Output Rule

In `trend mode`, the workflow should report:
- which platforms produced strong primary signals
- which platforms produced only weak or partial signals
- whether a topic is truly recent discussion momentum or just a long-running evergreen anxiety

## WeChat Keyword Hit-Article Mode

Use this mode when the user wants to know whether a keyword already has strong公众号 articles, what kinds of articles have spread well, or how to avoid rewriting stale angles.

### Purpose
This mode is for collecting publicly searchable high-performing公众号 article samples under a given keyword.
It is not a promise of a complete official leaderboard.
Treat it as `publicly visible hit-article sampling`, not `full-platform ranking`.

### Input
The user may provide:
- a keyword or topic phrase
- an optional time window
- an optional request to focus on titles, openings, structures, or differentiating angles

### Workflow
1. Search publicly searchable公众号 article pages for the keyword.
2. Prefer articles that appear to have strong spread signals, such as:
- repeated circulation across search results
- highly optimized and widely shared headline patterns
- recognizable public-account authors with strong distribution
- strong social quoting or secondary references
3. Extract from each article:
- title pattern
- opening move
- core framing
- repeatable structure
- emotionally sticky or shareable lines
4. Synthesize:
- what angles are already crowded
- what title formulas dominate
- what opening styles are overused
- what whitespace still exists for the user's positioning

### Output
Return:
1. publicly visible hit-article samples with links
2. title patterns
3. opening patterns
4. common structural patterns
5. stale or crowded angles
6. differentiated angles still worth writing
7. a recommendation on whether the keyword is still worth writing for the user's公众号
8. a `hit-evidence level` for each sample

### Hit-Evidence Levels
For every公众号 sample, label it with one of these levels:

- `Level 1: externally verified hit`
  Use only when there is explicit public evidence such as external ranking, public reposting that clearly states 10w+, or credible secondary references that identify the article as a hit piece.

- `Level 2: strong spread signal, unverified reading count`
  Use when the article shows multiple strong public signals such as repeated search visibility, strong headline optimization, recognizable distribution-heavy account, or strong secondary references, but no trustworthy public reading-count proof.

- `Level 3: relevant sample only`
  Use when the article is clearly relevant and useful for pattern analysis, but there is not enough public evidence to treat it as a hit article.

### Caveat Rule
Always state clearly that this mode retrieves `publicly searchable high-performing samples`, not a definitive internal WeChat leaderboard.
Do not imply access to complete reading-count or recommendation data unless a source explicitly provides it.
When in doubt, downgrade the evidence level rather than overstating certainty.


## Trend-To-Topic Derivation Logic

When the user wants to derive their own公众号 topics from public discussion momentum, do not jump directly from trends to imitation.
Use this three-stage derivation logic:

1. `Trends find emotions`
   - Identify what people are currently discussing
   - Extract the rising anxiety, lived state, or repeated confusion underneath the trend
   - Separate short-lived noise from durable emotional patterns

2. `Hit articles reveal platform packaging`
   - Find publicly searchable公众号 hit-like samples around the trend keyword
   - Extract title formulas, opening moves, framing choices, and crowded angles
   - Learn how the platform is packaging the issue, not what to copy

3. `Derive the user's differentiated topic`
   - Re-anchor the topic in the user's positioning and audience
   - Ask what existing articles are not yet saying clearly enough
   - Generate topics that match the user's voice, judgment style, and long-term content system

### Mandatory Filtering Layer
Before a trend becomes a candidate topic, check these three questions:
- Is this directly relevant to the user's audience?
- Can this become part of the user's long-term content line rather than a one-off trend reaction?
- Can the user add a deeper layer of explanation beyond what current hit articles are already saying?

If a trend does not pass these filters, do not recommend it as a primary topic.

### Output Preference
When using this derivation logic, prefer outputs in this order:
1. trend signal
2.公众号 hit-article packaging pattern
3. differentiated topic options for the user
4. recommended first topic to write

### Core Rule
Trends are for detecting external emotion.
Hit articles are for learning platform-native packaging.
The final topic must still be derived from the user's positioning, not from imitation.

## Internet Calibration Rule

Use internet calibration when local intuition is not enough.
The goal is not to outsource judgment.
The goal is to borrow mature external framing where it helps topic selection or mechanism naming.

### When To Calibrate
Default to lightweight web calibration when at least one of these is true:
- the topic is really about a psychological, organizational, or social mechanism
- the observation feels strong but the naming still feels soft or vague
- the topic is worth writing but may be too shallow without a stronger external frame
- multiple possible mechanisms exist and need sharper distinction
- the user explicitly asks whether a topic or writing angle has external precedent worth borrowing

### What To Search For
Search for these kinds of external material:
1. observation-to-idea or observation-to-insight workflows
2. ideation or brainstorm frameworks that help expand a phenomenon into multiple plausible mechanisms
3. creator, newsletter, or editorial frameworks for topic validation and audience resonance
4. essays, talks, or research that offer stronger naming for the mechanism beneath the phenomenon

### What Not To Do
- do not search just to find a ready-made answer
- do not copy another creator's final framing too closely
- do not replace lived texture with abstract outside theory
- do not let external references flatten the user's own voice or positioning

### How To Use The Results
Use internet calibration to improve one or more of these:
- mechanism naming
- topic selection confidence
- title framing
- opening path choice
- distinction between a main topic and a side example

External findings should sharpen the user's phenomenon-to-topic derivation, not override it.
