# Cross-Platform Routing Card

Use this card when routing one source asset across WeChat, Xiaohongshu, newsletter, or blog outputs.

## Core Rule

Do not treat cross-platform adaptation as a copy-shorter-copy-longer exercise.
Treat it as a role change:
- the source asset stays the same
- the platform job changes
- the reading behavior changes
- the packaging changes

## Default Platform Moves

| Platform Move | Typical Length | Paragraph Rhythm | Tone | Structural Priority | Visual Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| WeChat -> Xiaohongshu | 300-500 chars | 1-2 sentences per block | conversational, immediate | hook -> scene -> takeaway | first image is critical |
| WeChat -> Newsletter | around 70% of source | tighter than WeChat | more personal | keep the argument, compress the sections, add a light sender layer | optional |
| WeChat -> Blog | equal or longer than source | standard web-reading rhythm | analytical, explicit | add depth, examples, structure aids, and search-friendly framing | optional |

## Routing Rules

### WeChat -> Xiaohongshu
Use when:
- the WeChat article is stable
- the core insight can survive compression
- the platform goal is saves, search, or quick resonance

Do:
- compress to one hook, one scene, one takeaway
- keep only the strongest distinction or conflict
- convert into image-text coordination if needed

Do not:
- treat it as a shortened WeChat article
- keep long analytical transitions
- adapt before the source article is stable

### WeChat -> Newsletter
Use when:
- the article can benefit from a more direct sender-reader relationship
- the goal is repeat readership rather than public-account propagation

Do:
- keep the core argument
- shorten by default
- add a small personal layer when useful

Do not:
- keep every section from the WeChat article
- keep public-account pacing if it slows email reading

### WeChat -> Blog
Use when:
- the topic benefits from more explicit structure or technical detail
- the goal includes search, archive, or knowledge-base reuse

Do:
- expand examples or supporting detail when helpful
- add subheads or structure aids
- make the logic easy to skim on web

Do not:
- copy public-account paragraph rhythm blindly
- keep image or CTA logic that only makes sense inside WeChat

## Gate Checks Before Adaptation

Before adapting any source article, confirm:
1. the source article is already structurally stable
2. the article has a clear winning mechanism
3. the final draft has already passed the relevant editorial or quality gate
4. adaptation is serving a platform job, not just content recycling

## Quick Routing Questions

Before choosing a target platform, ask:
1. what is the platform supposed to win here: click, save, reply, archive, or search?
2. does this platform need a scene-first opening or a logic-first opening?
3. does the source argument survive compression, or does it require expansion?
4. is the image doing real work on this platform?
