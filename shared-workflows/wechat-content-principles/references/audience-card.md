# Audience Card

Use this card when a WeChat article needs to stay close to the intended reader instead of drifting into generic growth writing.

## Core Reader

Default core reader:
- ordinary office workers
- middle-career readers
- technical professionals
- 35+ technical executors, senior ICs, and edge-of-management readers

## What They Repeatedly Feel

Common hidden discomforts:
- busy all day, but little growth remains
- experienced, but unable to take much away
- relied on in the team, but unclear about long-term value
- always patching gaps, but rarely accumulating visible assets
- still useful in role, but unsure what remains outside the role

## What Their Daily Work Looks Like

Prefer scenes and actions like:
- requirements are vague, so they fill in the missing context
- boundaries are unclear, so they absorb the coordination cost
- cross-team work breaks, so they explain and reconnect the chain
- meetings end with agreement, but execution still drifts
- incidents happen, and they are expected to stabilize the mess
- they are the person who can still move things forward in ambiguity

## What They Secretly Fear

Deeper fears often include:
- effort is being mistaken for growth
- familiarity is being mistaken for transferable value
- experience is not turning into assets
- work is being consumed faster than it is being retained
- they may have done a lot without actually keeping much

## What Makes Them Feel Seen

Articles usually land better when they:
- name a hidden failure state clearly
- describe a familiar work action, not just an emotion
- explain why the reader feels stuck without humiliating them
- turn an abstract concern into a concrete mechanism
- ask a harder question at the end that the reader cannot dodge

## Red Flags

The article is drifting away from this reader if it:
- sounds like generic self-improvement
- talks about growth without technical or workplace texture
- uses only emotions and no work actions
- talks about mindset without structural friction
- offers motivation when the reader needs diagnosis

## Reader Test

Before finalizing, ask:
1. would this reader say `this is exactly my work state`
2. is there at least one familiar work action in the first half
3. is the problem described as a mechanism, not just a mood
4. does the ending send the reader back to their own working life
