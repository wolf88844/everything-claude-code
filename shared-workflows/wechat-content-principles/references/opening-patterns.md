# Opening Patterns

Use these when drafting or revising the first screen of a WeChat article.

## Pattern 1: Reclassification Opening

Shape:
- `People think the problem is X. It is actually Y.`
- `What feels uncomfortable is not X, but Y.`

Best for:
- hot-topic commentary
- diagnostic essays
- articles that need fast authority

## Pattern 2: Unease First

Shape:
- name the reader's discomfort first
- then say why the obvious explanation is too shallow

Best for:
- career anxiety
- middle-career readers
- topics with vague but recognizable pressure

## Pattern 3: Scene To Mechanism

Shape:
- start from a familiar scene
- quickly convert it into a mechanism

Best for:
- technical workplace writing
- meetings, handoffs, reviews, postmortems, vague requests

## Pattern 4: Surface Shock To Deeper Cost

Shape:
- acknowledge the news value
- then say the news is only the surface
- move to the more painful interpretation

Best for:
- trend pieces
- AI or tooling topics
- topics where the event itself is not the real point
