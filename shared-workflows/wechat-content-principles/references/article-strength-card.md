# Article Strength Card

Use this card when a WeChat article already feels strong and you want to capture why it works in a reusable way.

The goal is not to praise a finished article.
The goal is to extract writing behavior that future articles can repeat.

## What Made The Article Strong

A strong WeChat article often does all five:

1. it leads with the reader's real discomfort, not the event summary
2. it corrects the surface reading and pushes into a deeper mechanism
3. it builds one core distinction that can carry the whole article
4. it keeps deepening instead of restating
5. it ends by making the consequence harder to avoid

## Reusable Writing Rules

### Rule 1: Start By Reclassifying

Prefer an opening move like:
- `The real problem is not X. It is Y.`
- `What makes this uncomfortable is not X, but Y.`

This works because it does two jobs at once:
- it hooks the reader
- it establishes the account's judgment style

### Rule 2: Go One Layer Below The First Emotion

If the obvious reaction is:
- shock
- novelty
- fear
- anger

Ask what the deeper feeling is:
- humiliation
- instability
- a sense that nothing real was kept
- a sense that the reader has mistaken effort for asset

### Rule 3: Build One Core Distinction

Strong articles usually have one distinction that keeps returning:
- `did` vs `left behind`
- `familiarity` vs `asset`
- `self-understanding` vs `structural consequence`

If there is no clear distinction, the article is often still too loose.

### Rule 4: Progress By Reclassification

Each major section should do one of these:
- deepen the diagnosis
- raise the cost
- sharpen the distinction
- move from scene to mechanism
- move from personal discomfort to structural consequence

If a section only restates the same point in softer language, cut or compress it.

### Rule 5: Write Carrying Lines

A strong article needs lines that survive extraction.

Useful carry-line shapes:
- `Doing a lot does not mean leaving a lot behind.`
- `Familiarity in role is not the same as an asset you can take away.`
- `The real problem is not replacement. It is realizing you never kept what you built.`

### Rule 6: End By Adding Pressure

Do not end with:
- summary
- encouragement
- soft reflection

Prefer endings that:
- return the issue to the reader
- force a harder self-audit
- make the hidden cost newly visible

## First-Screen Checklist

Before finalizing the opening, check:
1. does it begin with discomfort instead of background
2. does it correct the obvious reading quickly
3. does it create a reason to continue beyond the event itself

## Middle-Section Checklist

Before finalizing the body, check:
1. what is the article's one core distinction
2. where does the article become deeper, not just longer
3. which paragraph first turns feeling into mechanism

## Ending Checklist

Before finalizing the ending, check:
1. what consequence becomes harder to ignore
2. what question gets pushed back onto the reader
3. does the ending feel sharper than the opening

## Capture Template

When a strong article is finished, record:
- best opening move
- deepest mechanism named
- core distinction
- strongest carry line
- section progression pattern
- ending pressure move

These six items should be reusable in future planning, drafting, and editing.
