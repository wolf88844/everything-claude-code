# Benchmark Reading Card

Use this card when reading strong competitor or reference articles.

The goal is not to copy the article.
The goal is to understand why it works and what can be borrowed safely.

## First-Screen Check

Ask:
1. what discomfort or scene appears first
2. what made the reader stop
3. did the opening start with scene, action, contradiction, or diagnosis

## Mechanism Check

Ask:
4. what hidden mechanism did this article name
5. what common misunderstanding did it correct
6. what core distinction carried the article

## Reader Fit Check

Ask:
7. what exact kind of reader does this article feel written for
8. what familiar actions or work scenes make it feel specific
9. what kind of hidden discomfort is being exposed

## Propagation Check

Ask:
10. which line is most likely to be screenshotted or forwarded
11. what title pattern is being used
12. how does the ending send the issue back to the reader

## Borrow Safely

Capture only these kinds of reusable assets:
- opening move
- mechanism naming style
- distinction pattern
- line shape
- ending move

Do not copy:
- exact phrasing at scale
- personal anecdotes that are not yours
- another creator's full framing without adaptation
