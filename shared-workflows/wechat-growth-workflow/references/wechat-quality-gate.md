# WeChat Quality Gate

Use this card before finalizing a WeChat article.

Do not judge the article only by smoothness.
Judge whether it actually lands with the intended reader.

## Opening

1. does the article begin with a real moment, scene, or discomfort
2. do the first 5 lines feel like `this is me`
3. does the opening avoid generic explanation

## Mechanism

4. has the article named a hidden failure state or discomfort clearly
5. has it moved from phenomenon to mechanism
6. is there one core distinction carrying the full article

## Reader Texture

7. does it include familiar work actions, not just moods
8. does it stay close to technical professionals or middle-career readers instead of drifting generic
9. does it hit an implicit failure point for 35+ technical executors or adjacent readers

## Propagation

10. is there at least one line worth screenshotting or forwarding
11. does the article contain a phrase the reader would repeat in their own words

## Ending

12. does the ending return the issue to the reader
13. does it create a new self-question instead of a soft summary
14. does the ending feel harder than the opening

## Verdict Guide

- `12 to 14 yes` -> strong, publishable
- `9 to 11 yes` -> workable, but should tighten before publishing
- `8 or fewer yes` -> still too weak or too generic

## Common Failure Modes

- generic growth writing
- concept-heavy, scene-light opening
- repeated description without deeper explanation
- weak reader specificity
- no quotable line
- soft ending that releases pressure
