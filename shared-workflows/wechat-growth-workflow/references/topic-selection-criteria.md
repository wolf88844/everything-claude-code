# Topic Selection Criteria

Use this reference to decide whether a选题 fits the user's公众号增长风格.

## Core Rule

A good topic for this account is not just useful.
It must produce both:
1. immediate recognition
2. a meaningful reframe

If a topic only teaches, it is usually too weak.
If a topic only vents emotion, it is usually too shallow.

## High-Fit Topic Traits

A strong topic usually has at least three of these:
- names a repeated internal struggle
- challenges a common self-explanation
- reduces shame while increasing clarity
- can be expressed as a clean contrast
- gives the reader language they want to save or share
- applies to many people but still feels personal
- creates follow-up topic opportunities

## Preferred Topic Shape

Best-performing topics often follow this pattern:
- visible behavior
- hidden structural reason

Examples:
- 总在定目标 -> 没有系统
- 一直很努力 -> 努力没有沉淀
- 反复开始 -> 没有积累结构
- 看了很多内容 -> 没有转成自己的表达

## Questions to Test a Topic

Before approving a topic, ask:
1. Will the reader immediately think "这是我"?
2. Is there a mistaken frame we can overturn?
3. Can the core idea be turned into one memorable sentence?
4. Does the topic avoid sounding like common productivity content?
5. Could this become a series rather than a one-off post?

If the answer to at least four is yes, the topic is likely strong.

## Preferred Topic Categories

These are high-fit categories for this voice:
- growth anxiety
- restarting behavior
- self-blame and misdiagnosis
- effort without accumulation
- emotion versus structure
- delayed growth and invisible progress
- content creation as accumulation
- work stress linked to inner narrative

## Lower-Fit Topics

These usually need reframing before use:
- generic efficiency hacks
- pure tool recommendations
- list-based self-improvement advice
- abstract life philosophy without a daily behavior anchor
- topics that depend on external trends rather than internal recognition

## Series Potential Test

Prefer topics that can branch into at least three follow-ups, such as:
- diagnosis post
- mechanism post
- system-building post
- self-observation post
- action/repair post

## Decision Rule

When in doubt, choose the topic with the strongest recognition line, not the topic with the most information.
