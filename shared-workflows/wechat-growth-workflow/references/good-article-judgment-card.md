# Good Article Judgment Card

Use this card when judging whether a draft, topic, or comparison result is moving toward a strong公众号 article rather than only a correct one.

## Core Principle

A strong article does not begin by proving the writer is right.
It begins by making the reader feel:
- this is me
- this is my scene
- this is the discomfort I could not explain

In short:
- first hit lived texture
- then name the hidden failure
- then explain the mechanism
- then push the problem back onto the reader

## What Strong Articles Usually Do

### 1. Hit reader texture before judgment

Do not begin by summarizing the topic or proving the argument.
Begin with:
- a real moment
- a repeated action
- a hidden discomfort
- a familiar fatigue
- a subtle humiliation or frustration

Goal:
Make the reader feel recognized before the article starts explaining anything.

### 2. Create a feeling of being "called out" rather than merely "agreeing"

Weak article reaction:
- this is true

Strong article reaction:
- yes, this is exactly it
- this is what I have been stuck in
- this finally says the thing I could not say well

The goal is not just correctness.
The goal is precise naming.

### 3. Stay close to a specific life, not broad self-improvement

Strong articles stay close to the lived reality of a narrow audience.
For this account, that usually means:
- middle-career technical professionals
- execution-layer workers
- people sliced up by requirements, coordination, rework, and patching
- people who are busy and important but still feel little growth
- people who have accumulated a lot but cannot carry much away

Do not drift into broad motivational writing.
Keep returning to how this kind of person actually lives and works.

## Structural Traits Of Strong Articles

### 4. The opening should have a moment, not just a topic

A strong opening usually enters through:
- a familiar move
- a concrete moment
- a repeated scene
- a quiet but recognizable turning point

Do not start by summarizing the article's theme.
Start by pulling the reader into a lived moment.

### 5. The middle must move from phenomenon to mechanism

Do not stop at surface description such as:
- tired
- busy
- no growth
- anxiety
- rule failure
- wanting to start over

A stronger middle section answers:
- why is this happening
- what hidden structure keeps recreating it
- what is the mechanism beneath the repeated discomfort

This is what turns the article from resonance into insight.

### 6. The ending should not only summarize; it should force self-return

Weak ending:
- therefore improve
- therefore change
- therefore learn X

Strong ending:
- what should you ask yourself now
- what is the deeper problem you actually need to face
- what have you been calling one thing that is really another thing

The ending should push the question back onto the reader.
A strong ending makes the reader ask: what about me then.

## Expression Traits Of Strong Articles

### 7. Name hidden discomforts

Strong articles often win by naming discomfort that is:
- subtle
- hard to explain
- easy to ignore
- familiar but under-described

This fits accounts that specialize in hidden friction and invisible failure points.

### 8. Use familiar actions, not only feelings

Readers are often hit harder by actions than by emotions.
Use repeated recognizable actions such as:
- buying books, courses, docs, or writing fresh-start plans
- learning another tool or framework and mistaking input for progress
- catching dropped work, patching unclear handoffs, explaining cross-team failures
- being lightly pushed back by "这种别提了" style organizational language
- having time on paper but no real leftover energy

Actions create immediate recognition.
Do not write only the feeling. Write the motion pattern.

### 9. Build at least one carry line

A strong article usually carries at least one line that can travel outside the body.
A carry line should be:
- short
- contrastive
- precise
- emotionally exact

Think of the article as building support for one sharper reusable line.

## The Four-Layer Win Pattern

Winning articles usually combine all four of these:
1. real scene
2. hidden naming
3. mechanism explanation
4. self-return question

If all four are present, the article is hard to make weak.

## Special Rule For AI And Trend Articles

When the topic is about AI, automation, role compression, or technology change, do not stop at a correct trend judgment.
A stronger version usually wins by exposing the reader's hidden career structure.

Weak trend version:
- AI is changing the industry
- execution work will be compressed
- people need to upgrade

Stronger version:
- what the reader thought they were being paid for is not the full truth
- the reader's most stable value may already be the layer getting repriced first
- the tool is upgrading, but the reader's role may still be stuck in the same place
- the real danger is not the tool itself, but years of staying in the "defined by others, executed by me" position

For this kind of article, prefer this sequence:
1. start from the reader's real unease
2. expose what layer of value has actually been paying them
3. explain why that layer is being repriced first
4. force the reader to ask whether their role has upgraded, not only their tools

Useful line shapes for this kind of article:
- the dangerous part is not X; it is Y
- what used to make you stable is now what gets repriced first
- the tool upgraded, but the role did not
- being useful is not the same as being safe
- the organization is not only getting faster; it is revaluing what counts as scarce

What to avoid:
- generic AI commentary
- tool tips pretending to be career diagnosis
- ending with only "learn AI"
- discussing replacement without naming the reader's actual role structure
## Writer Fit For This Account

This account is strongest when writing:
- mechanism-driven viewpoint essays
- hidden-friction diagnosis
- invisible failure naming
- structure-level explanation

It is less naturally strong when writing:
- generic method posts
- listicle-style tutorials
- broad motivation pieces
- abstract advice with thin lived texture

## Draft Check Questions

Before approving a draft, ask:
1. does the opening begin with a real moment instead of explanation
2. will the target reader feel "this is me" within the first five lines
3. has the article named a vague state more precisely than the reader could
4. has it moved from phenomenon to mechanism
5. has it used recognizable actions, not only feelings
6. does it produce at least one line worth saving or sharing
7. is it close enough to the target audience's actual work and life
8. does it hit a hidden failure point for a 35+ technical executor or similar reader
9. does the ending press the question back onto the reader
10. does the article create a new self-question after reading

If roughly seven or more of these are clearly true, the draft is usually on strong ground.

## House Writing Formula

When in doubt, use this formula:
- start from a moment this reader knows well but has not fully explained
- name the hidden failure or hidden discomfort inside it
- explain the organizational or psychological mechanism beneath it
- push the question back onto the reader at the end
