# WeChat Success Engineering Card

Use this card to define what a topic is supposed to win before drafting, and to keep that win-condition visible during writing and review.

## Core Rule

Do not ask only:
- is this topic good
- is this article well written

Ask instead:
- what is this piece supposed to win
- is this topic naturally suited to that outcome
- does the draft actually build toward that outcome
- what would count as success after publishing

## Success Spec

Before drafting, every article should have a short `Success Spec` with these fields:

1. `Primary goal`
Choose one:
- recognition
- spread
- relationship
- conversion

2. `Expected level`
Choose one:
- S -> identity breakthrough piece
- A -> stable hit for the core audience
- B -> expansion or testing piece
- C -> support / rhythm / card piece

3. `Why this topic can win`
State why this topic is naturally suited to the chosen goal.
Do not force a topic to carry a goal it is weak at.

4. `Minimum success signal`
Define the weakest acceptable signal.
Examples:
- readers start summarizing who this account is
- comments repeat the key mechanism in their own words
- people forward the piece using the same shorthand line
- the article creates stronger trust, not just broad agreement

5. `Most likely failure mode`
Examples:
- too abstract too early
- broad resonance but weak identity reinforcement
- technically right but not quotable enough
- strong recognition but weak spread trigger

## Goal Matching Rule

Different goals require different structures.

### If the goal is `recognition`
Writing should strengthen:
- who this account is for
- what hidden problem it explains well
- what mother theme it belongs to

### If the goal is `spread`
Writing should strengthen:
- a sharper first-screen hook
- one screenshot-worthy line
- one easy-to-retell reframe

### If the goal is `relationship`
Writing should strengthen:
- emotional accuracy
- deeper naming of the reader's long-term friction
- a sense that the writer understands more than surface pain

### If the goal is `conversion`
Writing should strengthen:
- why the reader needs a stronger framework, not just resonance
- why the writer's lens compounds over time
- one step from article insight toward long-term trust

## Writing-Time Reference Rule

The `Success Spec` is not only for planning.
It must remain visible during:
- outlining
- drafting
- editorial pass
- final selection between drafts

If two versions are available, choose the one that better serves the stated success goal, not simply the smoother one.

## Topic Suitability Check

Before investing in a topic, ask:
1. Is this topic naturally recognizable as mine?
2. Is it fresh enough relative to recent posts?
3. Does it connect back to one of the IP pillars?
4. Does it suit the goal I am assigning to it?
5. Is it worth occupying one publishing slot at this stage?

## After-Publish Connection

After publishing, the feedback loop should review the same fields:
- did the primary goal actually happen
- did the minimum success signal appear
- was the failure mode correctly predicted
- should this topic type be repeated, upgraded, or reduced
