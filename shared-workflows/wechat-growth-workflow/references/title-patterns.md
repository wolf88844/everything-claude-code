# Title Patterns

Use this card when generating WeChat title options.

The goal is not to brainstorm endlessly.
The goal is to choose a title shape that matches the mechanism and reader pressure of the article.

## Pattern 1: Not X, But Y

Best for:
- sharp reclassification
- hidden mechanism topics
- articles with a strong diagnosis

Examples:
- `让人难受的，不是X，而是Y`
- `真正卡住你的，不是X，而是Y`

## Pattern 2: Many People Are Not..., They Are...

Best for:
- common misunderstanding topics
- high-recognition reader pain
- career and growth articles

Examples:
- `很多人不是没有时间，而是没有余电`
- `很多技术人不是经验不够，而是经验没有升级`

## Pattern 3: Why ... The Real Problem Is Not ...

Best for:
- mechanism-driven titles
- articles that need authority and interpretation

Examples:
- `为什么很多人...，真正的问题不是...`
- `为什么...真正让人难受的，不是...`

## Pattern 4: Did vs Left Behind

Best for:
- experience, asset, growth, and second-curve topics
- articles about value that can or cannot be carried away

Examples:
- `做过，不等于留下`
- `做过很多，并不等于留下很多`

## Pattern 5: Scene-Led Pressure

Best for:
- titles that need stronger lived texture
- technical workplace topics

Examples:
- `开完会大家都点头，为什么事情还是会跑偏`
- `需求没讲清、边界没对齐，为什么最后总是你来补`

## Pattern 6: Reader Audit

Best for:
- endings that return the issue to the reader
- articles designed to trigger self-questioning

Examples:
- `你最该问自己的，不是X，而是Y`
- `你这些年，到底留下了什么`

## Title Check

Before finalizing a title, ask:
1. does it name a discomfort or a mechanism
2. does it feel specific to the target reader
3. does it sound like a judgment, not a category label
4. would it still make sense if quoted alone
