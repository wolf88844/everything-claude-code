# WeChat Article Scorecard

Use this scorecard after drafting any公众号 article.
The goal is not to chase abstract literary quality. The goal is to judge whether the piece is strong for this user's定位, audience, and platform.

## Scoring Rule

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.
- 1 = weak
- 2 = below bar
- 3 = acceptable
- 4 = strong
- 5 = excellent

After scoring all dimensions:
- 32-40 = publish-ready
- 24-31 = promising but needs revision
- 16-23 = weak, should be reworked
- below 16 = wrong angle or weak execution

## Dimensions

### 1. Audience Precision
Question: does this clearly feel written for experienced technical professionals stuck in the execution layer?
Look for:
- technical-worker life structure
- execution-layer frustration
- family or energy pressure when relevant
- not a generic self-help tone

### 2. Opening Hit Rate
Question: does the first screen make the target reader feel "this is about me"?
Look for:
- concrete lived state
- fast recognition
- no slow warm-up
- no abstract lecture opening

### 3. Problem Diagnosis
Question: does the article explain the real problem more deeply than the reader's default explanation?
Look for:
- a false but attractive explanation is named
- a deeper explanation is introduced
- the reframing feels earned, not forced

### 4. Insight Density
Question: are there enough sharp judgments worth underlining, screenshotting, or forwarding?
Look for:
- quotable lines
- non-obvious but clear judgments
- more than one insight turn, not just one idea repeated

### 5. Platform Fit
Question: does this feel like a公众号 article rather than a lecture note, blog post, or thread?
Look for:
- recognition before instruction
- calm but readable rhythm
- shareable emotional accuracy
- suitable long-form paragraph pacing

### 6. Differentiation
Question: does this add something beyond common公众号 growth/career writing?
Look for:
- a fresh angle
- a sharper target reader
- stronger diagnosis than generic advice
- clear distance from stale listicles and productivity sermons

### 7. Conversion Of Experience
Question: does the article naturally connect experience to judgment, expression, structure, or assets?
Look for:
- not just describing pain
- not just offering comfort
- movement toward the user's core positioning

### 8. Ending Power
Question: does the ending leave the reader with a clear internal question, tension, or next thought?
Look for:
- not a neat summary dump
- not a motivational slogan
- preferably a self-diagnostic closing question

## Mandatory Output Format

After drafting, always return:
1. total score
2. score by dimension
3. 1-3 strongest parts
4. 1-3 weakest parts
5. final verdict: publish-ready / revise / re-angle

## Red Flags

If any of these appear, lower the score:
- could apply to any white-collar worker with no technical specificity
- too abstract in the first 20 percent
- too much concept, too little life texture
- too much advice, too little diagnosis
- ends with generic encouragement instead of a sharper inner question
