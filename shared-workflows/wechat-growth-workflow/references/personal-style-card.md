# Personal Writing Style Card

This reference captures the preferred公众号写作风格 derived from the user's approved sample article.

## Positioning

This voice is for WeChat public-account essays aimed at growth-oriented but emotionally tired readers.

The writing should feel:
- perceptive
- restrained
- emotionally accurate
- quietly sharp
- easy to quote

It should not feel:
- preachy
- motivational
- managerial
- overly instructional
- like generic self-help content

## Core Effect

The article should make the reader feel two things in sequence:
1. "This is exactly what I do."
2. "I was explaining my problem the wrong way."

The piece works when the reader feels understood first, then reframed.

For broad-growth essays, recognition should come from lived scenes before abstract judgment.
The reader should see their own evening, weekend, exhaustion, avoidance, or half-empty state before they are given a concept.

## Signature Moves

### 1. Start from repeated reader behavior

Open from something the reader repeatedly does, not from an abstract thesis.

Examples of this move:
- repeatedly setting goals
- repeatedly restarting plans
- repeatedly reorganizing life after feeling bad

The point is to create instant recognition.

When possible, upgrade this from repeated behavior to repeated lived scene:
- 下班以后理论上还有两个小时
- 周末理论上还有半天
- 真正坐下来，只想刷手机、发呆、躺着

These scenes are often stronger than a clean conceptual opening.

### 2. Reframe the hidden problem

The preferred pattern is:
- people think the problem is A
- the deeper problem is B

Examples:
- not unclear goals, but no stable system
- not lack of effort, but lack of accumulation
- not no action, but no carry-over structure
- not no time, but no usable energy

### 3. Use contrast-heavy progression

A key sentence pattern in this voice is:
- 很多人不是……而是……
- 你以为自己缺的是……其实更常见的是……
- 表面上看……往深里看……

These contrasts are not decoration. They are the engine of the argument.

### 4. Build depth section by section

Each section should deepen the previous one.

Typical progression:
- visible behavior
- common misunderstanding
- structural explanation
- cost of missing structure
- what stable growth actually depends on

Avoid flattening the article into parallel tips.

For mainstream readers, prefer this order:
- lived state
- emotional truth
- hidden mechanism
- structural explanation
- reframed conclusion

Do not begin with the mechanism if the lived state has not been earned first.

### 5. End with a lingering self-diagnostic line

The ending should not sound like a recap.

Prefer:
- a final contrast
- a self-question
- a recognition line the reader can sit with

## Rhythm and Paragraphing

- Keep paragraphs short.
- Mix one-line paragraphs with slightly longer explanatory blocks.
- Use deliberate repetition when it increases emotional recognition.
- Use standalone pivot lines such as:
  - 这才是真正的问题。
  - 这就是很多人最熟悉的循环。
  - 说到底，问题不在……

The reading rhythm should feel breathable on mobile.

Prefer concrete life slices over summary language.

Stronger:
- 下班以后，剩下那点时间，只够恢复，不够生长。

Weaker:
- many people lack sufficient post-work energy for long-term development.

## Language Preferences

Prefer words like:
- 重启
- 积累
- 留下来
- 接上
- 沉淀
- 结构
- 系统
- 生长
- 余电
- 恢复
- 耗空

Prefer plain language over abstract jargon.

Avoid words that feel too course-like or managerial, such as:
- 底层逻辑 when overused
- 方法论 when a simpler word works
- 认知升级 if it sounds inflated
- 执行效率 unless clearly needed
- 优化/最大化 when plain verbs are stronger

## Emotional Tone

The voice should never humiliate the reader.

It can be sharp, but the sharpness should be aimed at the mistaken frame, not at the person.

The reader should feel:
- relieved
- exposed in a useful way
- less ashamed
- more clear about what is actually wrong
- this writer understands what my life actually feels like
- my exhaustion is being named, not judged

## Structural Template

Use this default sequence for similar essays:
1. Familiar behavior
2. Why that behavior feels useful in the moment
3. Why it keeps failing
4. What the real missing piece is
5. What changes when a person has that missing piece
6. Closing question or recognition line

For broad middle-class / working adult topics, prefer this variant:
1. Concrete life scene
2. Hidden emotional truth inside that scene
3. The mistaken public explanation
4. The deeper explanation
5. What this means for future growth
6. Closing recognition or self-diagnostic question

## Quotable Line Test

A draft is close to target when it contains several lines that can stand alone in a screenshot or highlight, such as:
- 目标给的是希望。系统给的，才是生长。
- 很多人不是没方向，是一直在重启自己。
- 真正的问题，不是努力太少，而是努力没有留下来。

For this voice, the quotable lines should come from both:
- judgment lines
- lived-state lines

Examples of lived-state lines:
- 不是没有时间，是终于有了时间，自己已经空了。
- 剩下的，只够恢复，不够生长。
- 不是不会安排，是根本提不起那口气。

## Banned Outcomes

Do not let the article become:
- a listicle full of tips
- a productivity sermon
- a framework dump too early
- a motivational pep talk
- a research summary with no emotional through-line
- a clean concept article with too little life texture

## How to Use This Reference

When the user asks for my style, 按我喜欢的风格, or provides an approved sample as a reference:
- read this file before drafting
- preserve the calm, contrast-led, insight-driven structure
- optimize for recognition and quotability
- prefer lived scenes before judgment when the topic is broad and realistic
- end with a question, not a neat summary

## Fixed Positioning

Primary audience:
- technical professionals with around 10 years of experience
- especially those around age 35
- long stuck at the execution layer
- under real-life pressure such as family responsibility and limited energy outside work

Core mission:
- help them improve judgment
- help them improve expression
- help them build a sustainable growth structure
- help them turn years of experience into content and durable assets
- help them gradually build a second growth curve outside the job itself

Core positioning sentence:
- help execution-layer technical professionals turn experience into judgment, judgment into expression, and expression into assets

Topic preference implication:
- prefer topics about technical professionals whose experience is not yet upgrading
- prefer topics about why execution-heavy careers stop compounding
- prefer topics about judgment, expression, structure, transferable ability, and second growth curves
- avoid generic self-help topics that do not clearly connect to the life structure of experienced technical workers
