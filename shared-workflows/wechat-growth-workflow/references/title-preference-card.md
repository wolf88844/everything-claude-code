# Title Preference Card

Use this reference to write titles in the user's preferred growth-essay style.

## Core Principle

The best titles do not merely describe the topic.
They reframe the reader's self-explanation.

A strong title should make the reader feel one of these:
- I do this
- I have been naming this wrong
- this explains why I keep failing

## Best Title Patterns

### 1. Misdiagnosis Reversal
- 你不是不自律，你只是没有一个能托住自己的系统
- 你不是不想成长，你只是一直在重启自己

### 2. Hidden Cause
- 为什么你总在定目标，却越来越焦虑
- 为什么你一直很努力，却总觉得自己没长出来

### 3. Contrast Line
- 目标给你希望，系统才让你生长
- 热血能推你一阵子，系统才能托你很多年

### 4. Familiar Pain + Better Explanation
- 你每次都很认真，为什么还是坚持不下去
- 你不是做得不够多，而是做过的东西没留下来

## Title Writing Rules

- Prefer one clean insight over keyword stuffing.
- Make the emotional friction visible.
- Avoid sounding like a course or office training.
- Avoid vague positive titles.
- Prefer titles that remove shame while increasing clarity.

## Title Traits to Favor

- recognizability
- emotional precision
- contrast
- diagnosis
- quotability

## Title Traits to Avoid

- flat summaries
- empty inspiration
- too much jargon
- long academic phrasing
- generic "how to" framing unless the user wants it

## Useful Sentence Frames

- 你不是……你只是……
- 为什么你总是……却……
- 很多人以为……其实……
- 真正拖垮你的，不是……而是……
- 你以为自己缺的是……其实更缺的是……

## Title Evaluation Test

A title is strong if it passes at least three of these:
1. The reader can see themselves in it immediately.
2. The title suggests a deeper explanation.
3. The title can trigger shares or saves.
4. The tone feels sharp but not humiliating.
5. The idea does not sound overused.

## Output Guidance

When generating title options:
- include at least 10
- include a mix of softer and sharper versions
- rank the top 3 by likely click appeal and fit with the user's voice
