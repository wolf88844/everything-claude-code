## One-Line Rule

On公众号, a strong article usually wins by making the reader feel understood before making the writer look correct.

## Core Writing Order

Use this order for broad work, growth, burnout, and career topics:
1. familiar life state
2. the reader's usual explanation
3. why that explanation feels persuasive
4. why it is incomplete
5. the deeper frame
6. a closing question or recognition line

If a draft starts with the polished conclusion too early, it is often less platform-fit.

## What公众号 Rewards Most

- recognition before instruction
- life texture before abstract summary
- shareable judgment before neutral information
- emotionally accurate diagnosis before advice
- strong first screen even in long-form writing

## First-Screen Test

The first screen should do at least one of these:
- describe a real daily state
- surface a hidden emotional truth
- name a misunderstanding the reader already lives inside

If the first screen only presents a concept, the draft is probably too cold for公众号.

## Draft Selection Rule

If two drafts express the same idea:
- choose the one with stronger recognition
- choose the one with more lived scenes
- choose the one that better explains the attraction of the wrong explanation
- choose the one more likely to be forwarded with "这篇说得对"

Do not automatically choose the cleanest concept draft.

## Practical Decision Questions

Before drafting, ask:
1. What exact life state should the opening recreate?
2. What wrong explanation is the reader likely using?
3. Why does that wrong explanation feel emotionally attractive?
4. What is the deeper frame?
5. Which line is most likely to be screenshotted or shared?

## Things to Avoid

- polished but cold concept openings
- consultant tone
- framework dumping too early
- generic self-help motivation
- summaries with weak life texture
- writing that sounds correct but not lived-in

## Platform-Fit Signal

A draft is likely公众号-native if the reader can quickly feel:
- this is about my life
- this explains why I keep thinking about it this way
- this gives me a better way to understand myself

## Topic-Type Rule: Growth-Curve And Transition Topics

When the topic is about second growth curves, changing tracks, career transition, experience upgrade, or transferable ability:

Do not start by announcing the correct insight too early.
Start by reconstructing the reader's mistaken path.

Preferred order:
1. show how the reader naturally enters the wrong explanation
2. explain why that explanation feels persuasive and emotionally attractive
3. show how that path creates more confusion, emptiness, or false motion
4. only then introduce the deeper diagnosis
5. re-anchor the solution in what the reader already has but has not yet named, extracted, or upgraded

Why this matters on公众号:
- readers are more likely to continue when they feel their internal confusion has been accurately reconstructed
- a correct conclusion without the mistaken path often feels like a lecture
- a reconstructed mistaken path makes the final insight feel earned, not imposed

What to prefer:
- "why you keep looking outward"
- "why this explanation is so tempting"
- "why you are getting more confused instead of more clear"
- "what you already have but have not yet seen"

What to avoid:
- opening with the final answer too fast
- abstract concept-first framing
- acting as if the reader's mistaken path is naive or shallow

## Full-Sample Drafting Rule: Body-Level Fit

Before drafting, assume broad growth topics are weak until translated into a technical scene.

Preferred drafting path:
1. open with a meeting, review, blame, rework, alignment failure, or decision-pressure scene
2. identify the hidden friction in that scene
3. rename the scene into a deeper growth, role, or organizational problem
4. escalate into structural consequence
5. close by naming the deeper cost

Do not trust a draft just because the diagnosis is correct.
For this account, correct-but-broad is currently weaker than scene-led-and-sharp.

## Topic-Type Rule: Bias, Misreading, Self-Justification, And Rule Distortion Topics

When the topic is about self-serving interpretation, hearing things the wrong way, defensive understanding, selective listening, or judgment distortion:

Do not begin with workplace mechanism analysis by default.
If a compact, vivid, repeatable life story exists, prefer opening with that story first.

Preferred order:
1. start with a small story, image, or scene that makes the mechanism instantly visible
2. let the reader understand the mechanism before naming the concept
3. only then move from the story into the adult version of the same behavior
4. upgrade the behavior into a deeper diagnosis about bias, self-protection, or distorted judgment
5. close on the cost: what this pattern damages in growth, relationships, or collaboration

Why this works on公众号:
- readers stop more easily for a vivid scene than for an abstract explanation
- a memorable story gives the article a reusable share-handle
- understanding first, self-recognition second, diagnosis third is often stronger than mechanism-first framing

What to prefer:
- a child's action that mirrors adult cognition
- a small daily misunderstanding with strong visual memory
- a scene that can be retold in one sentence
- a story that naturally upgrades into a broader adult truth

What to avoid:
- starting with organizational theory when the mechanism can be shown more simply
- opening with the clean conclusion too early
- turning a psychologically vivid topic into a dry collaboration explainer in the first screen

## Empirical Rule: Story-Led Openings For Broad Human Patterns

For broad human-pattern topics, especially these:
- self-justification
- self-serving bias
- misreading rules
- hearing feedback as attack
- turning facts into self-protective explanations

A story-led opening may outperform a direct work-scene opening when:
1. the mechanism is easier to understand through a vivid small story
2. the story can be naturally upgraded into adult work and relationship behavior
3. the story gives the reader a memorable shorthand for the whole article

Use this test before drafting:
- if a work scene explains the problem better, use the work scene
- if a short story makes the mechanism instantly visible and broadly shareable, use the story first

Do not treat story-led openings as default for every article.
Use them when they increase memory, recognition, and forwarding potential.

## Topic-Type Rule: Restart, Self-Renewal, And Change-Feeling Topics

When the topic is about restarting, self-renewal, repeated planning, wanting to change, or the illusion of growth:

Do not begin by stating the final diagnosis too quickly.
Prefer reconstructing the pleasure loop first.

Preferred order:
1. open with a small, highly familiar action such as making a plan, buying a book, opening a new doc, or saying "from today on"
2. show why that moment feels relieving, hopeful, or morally repairing
3. distinguish change-feeling from actual change
4. explain why real change feels slower, duller, and less emotionally rewarding
5. close on the deeper question: is the reader producing change, or just producing the feeling of change?

Why this works on公众号:
- readers often do not just need the diagnosis, they need the loop they are trapped in to be reconstructed
- these topics spread better when the article explains why restarting is emotionally addictive
- broad self-improvement articles are weaker than mechanism articles about self-comfort, restart loops, and false motion

What to prefer:
- concrete restart rituals
- the emotional payoff of "I am starting again"
- the difference between activation and accumulation
- the contrast between immediate relief and delayed growth

What to avoid:
- generic anti-procrastination framing
- self-discipline lectures
- method lists too early
- treating repeated restarting as only a willpower problem

## Empirical Rule: Change-Feeling Can Outperform Change-Talk

For articles about wanting to change, the stronger version may not be the one that says "people do not really change" the earliest.
The stronger version is often the one that first shows:
- why restart rituals feel so good
- why people keep returning to them
- how change-feeling replaces actual change

Use this test before drafting:
- if the article is only saying "you are addicted to restarting," it may feel thin
- if the article reconstructs the reader's restart loop, emotional payoff, and false progress feeling, it becomes more readable and more shareable

## Topic-Type Rule: Workplace Grievance To Organizational Reality Topics

When a topic begins from one concrete workplace grievance, such as being lightly pushed back, being told not to bring something up, or feeling wronged by a vague managerial signal:

Do not stop at the grievance itself.
The stronger公众号 version usually upgrades from a single hurt moment into an explanation of how the organization actually operates.

Preferred order:
1. open with the precise awkward or hurtful moment
2. name what felt uncomfortable about it
3. show why the discomfort is bigger than one incident
4. reframe the event as an organizational mechanism, default order, or real boundary
5. close on the deeper cost: what kind of worker loses repeatedly inside this system

Why this works on公众号:
- readers may not share the exact same incident, but they often recognize the same organizational logic
- a grievance alone creates sympathy; a mechanism creates forwarding and saving
- the strongest version turns "this hurt" into "this is how companies really run"

What to prefer:
- one familiar workplace moment
- one sharp phrase that names the hidden rule
- a fast upgrade from event to organizational logic
- default order, boundary, interpretation power, or atmosphere as the deeper frame

What to avoid:
- staying too long inside one complaint
- writing only from personal unfairness
- turning the article into emotional venting before the mechanism is named


## Success Engineering Hook

Before drafting, pair every article decision with a short success judgment:
1. what is this article mainly trying to win: recognition, spread, relationship, or conversion
2. is this topic naturally suited to that goal
3. what is the minimum signal that would count as success
4. what is the most likely failure mode

Do not let a topic carry a goal it is weak at.
A strong recognition topic may be only average for spread.
A bridge article may build trust without producing breakout traffic.
Judge accordingly.

## Execution-Layer Positioning Rule

When the article is about why an execution-layer reader is not moving up, do not jump too early to advice like "you need better expression" or "you need to learn to define problems".

Prefer this order:
1. recreate the felt frustration of doing more, getting more stable, but not moving up
2. explain why more execution value does not automatically become position change
3. separate execution value from judgment value
4. only then land on the upgrade direction: speaking clearly, defining problems, clarifying tradeoffs, making judgment visible

For this topic family, explanation of the structural stall must come before the upgrade prescription.
Otherwise the piece becomes correct but flatter, and reads like generic career advice.
